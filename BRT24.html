Second Test Day 1 Highlights

The first day of the second Test in the Botham-Richards Trophy 2024 series saw England dominate with a robust batting display. After the early dismissal of Zak Crawley for a duck, Ben Duckett spearheaded England’s recovery with an aggressive knock of 71 runs from 59 balls, including 14 boundaries. His partnership with Ollie Pope laid a solid foundation, as Pope went on to score a magnificent 121, his sixth Test century, despite being dropped twice by the West Indies fielders.

Joe Root contributed 14 runs before falling to Jayden Seales, while Harry Brook added a quick 36 from 34 balls before being dismissed by Kevin Sinclair, who celebrated with his signature somersault. Ben Stokes, the England captain, also played a crucial role, scoring 69 runs and partnering effectively with Pope.

The West Indies bowlers faced a tough challenge against England’s aggressive batting strategy, known as 'Bazball'. Alzarri Joseph was the standout bowler for the visitors, claiming three wickets, while Jayden Seales and Kevin Sinclair took two wickets each. The West Indies' fielding, however, left much to be desired, with several crucial catches being dropped, which allowed England to amass a significant total.

By the end of the day, England had reached 416 runs in 88.3 overs, setting a strong platform for the rest of the match. The West Indies will need to put in a solid batting performance on day two to stay competitive in the game.
