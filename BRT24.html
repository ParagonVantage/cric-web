Third Test Day 1 Highlights - July 26, 2024

The opening day of the third Test in the Botham-Richards Trophy 2024 series witnessed a compelling contest between England and the West Indies. Batting first, the West Indies put up a total of 282 runs. Kraigg Brathwaite anchored the innings with a determined 61, and Jason Holder contributed a crucial 59. Despite losing wickets at regular intervals, the West Indies managed to reach a competitive score, thanks to important lower-order contributions.

England's bowlers had a mixed day. Gus Atkinson was the standout performer, claiming four wickets and maintaining pressure on the West Indies batsmen. He was well-supported by Chris Woakes, who picked up three wickets, and Mark Wood, who chipped in with two important dismissals. Shoaib Bashir, although not among the wicket-takers, bowled economically to keep the run rate in check.

In their reply, England faced early setbacks, ending the day at 38 for 3. Zak Crawley, Ben Duckett, and Joe Root were dismissed cheaply as the West Indies bowlers, led by Alzarri Joseph and Jayden Seales, made significant inroads into the English top order. Joseph and Seales each took a wicket, while Shannon Gabriel added another to leave England reeling.

With England trailing by 244 runs and seven wickets in hand, Day 2 promises to be a crucial one in determining the direction of this Test match. Both teams will look to seize control, with England aiming to build a substantial first-innings total and the West Indies striving to capitalize on their strong start.
